# Stoik Product Strategy

## Consumables That Work at This Scale

### The Rule of Consumables That Work
Before listing ideas, lock these hard constraints. A consumable works only if it satisfies at least 3 of these 5:
1. Degrades silently over time.
2. Hygiene or health-adjacent.
3. Annoying to re-buy manually.
4. Standardized and predictable.
5. Emotionally invisible.

White shirts fit this perfectly.

### Tier 1 — Very Strong (Stoik-core compatible)
1. Socks (premium, identical pairs).
2. Underwear.
3. Towels (bath + face).
4. Bedsheets and pillowcases.

### Tier 2 — Health-first, low-fashion
1. Deodorant (clinical, neutral).
2. Oral care (toothbrush heads, floss).
3. Skincare basics (cleanser, moisturizer, sunscreen).

### Tier 3 — Quiet luxury, long-term
1. Razor blades and grooming basics.
2. Sleep aids (magnesium sachets, herbal teas, eye masks).

### What Not to Do
Avoid trend-based products, seasonal fashion, high-identity items, or anything that requires taste.

### The Real Insight
You are not building products. You are building a system that quietly removes decay from daily life.

### How Amazon, Apple, Netflix Think About This
1. Amazon: friction removal and logistics dominance.
2. Apple: calm UX, trust, ecosystem lock-in.
3. Netflix: predictability and zero effort.

Stoik should feel closer to Apple + Netflix than Amazon.

## Fundability Reality Check

### What Investors Actually Fund
They fund evidence of inevitability. That evidence usually looks like:
1. People already paying.
2. People sticking around.
3. Unit economics that scale.
4. A wedge that expands.
5. Founder clarity and discipline.

### Is White or Grey Shirt Subscription Fundable
Yes, because the behavior change is: "I do not think about replacing basics anymore."

### The Real Wedge
You are selling predictability, hygiene confidence, reduced cognitive load, and baseline stability.

### Why Starting Narrow Helps
"We are only doing white, black, and grey shirts until this works" signals discipline, focus, and taste.

### What Funding Is Realistic
Likely:
1. Angel investors.
2. Operator angels.
3. Seed funds that like boring businesses.
4. Subscription, consumer infrastructure, and retention-focused funds.

Not likely early:
1. Large VC rounds.
2. Hype-driven consumer funds.
3. Growth-at-all-costs capital.

### Minimum Fundable Proof
1. Small group paying monthly.
2. Evidence of low churn.
3. Clear replacement cadence.
4. Clean unit economics.
5. An "inevitable" story, not a flashy one.

### What Would Kill This
Fashion framing, too many SKUs early, loud marketing, complex UX, or lifestyle branding too soon.

### When to Raise
Not yet. Build the quiet, boring version first. Use funding as leverage, not oxygen.
